What We Sell

We have all kinds of drying racks so you can find the one that's right for you:

1. Folding Racks

If you have a small house, apartment, or room, these are the way to go. They don't weigh much, you can move them around, and you can stash them away when you're done. They're great for delicates like silk, lingerie, and sweaters. They'll dry your clothes gently without stretching them out or ruining them.

2. Winged Racks

These have wings that pop out, so you get more space to dry stuff. They're good if you have a lot of clothes or big things like blankets and coats. They're made strong, so they won't tip over when you load them up.

3. Wall-Mounted and Racks

If you don't have a lot of space on the floor, try these! You can fold them up against the wall or tuck them away when you don't need them. They're perfect for balconies, bathrooms, or laundry rooms. They're handy and help you save space.

4. Ceiling-Mounted Airers

These are great if you have tall ceilings. You use a rope to pull them up and down so you can hang your clothes. They have a bunch of bars to hang stuff on, so you can dry a lot without taking up floor space.

5. Heated Airers

If you want your clothes to dry faster when it's cold or rainy, these are for you. They use electricity to dry your clothes without using a lot of power, unlike those big dryers. They're easy on your clothes and can save you money on your electric bill.

Some Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of our products:

Don't Cram: Leave some space between your clothes so the air can flow and they'll dry faster.

Move Things Around: Turn your clothes every so often so they don't dry unevenly and start to smell.

Find a Breezy Spot: Put your rack near a window, vent, or fan so your clothes will dry quickly.

Keep It Clean: Wipe down your rack every now and then to keep it from getting dusty and gross.

Spin It: Before you hang your clothes up, spin them in the washing machine to get rid of as much water as possible.
